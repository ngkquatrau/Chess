Placement
Both players start with one Queen located in the center of the board next to the King. White's Queen starts on d1 while Black's Queen starts on d8.
A good way to memorize the placements of the King and Queen is to follow a popular mnemonic: "The Queen gets her color." which means that the White Queen should be located on a White square and vice versa. It is important to note that this only works if the chessboard is correctly oriented.

Movement
The Queen can move any number of unoccupied squares in a straight line vertically, horizontally or diagonally. It is the combination of the movement of the Rook and the Bishop.

Queen Development
A player should generally delay from developing the Queen as developing it too quickly can expose it to attacks by enemy pieces, which causes the player to lose turns and/or time removing the Queen from danger. Despite this, beginners often develop the Queen very early in the game hoping to annihalite the opponent's position and deliver an early checkmate.

Queen Exchange
A Queen exchange often marks the beginning of the endgame, though there are Queen endgames, and there are cases where Queens are exchange at the beginning of the game. In all situations it is still common to try to promote a Pawn to a Queen to ensure a set of strong pieces and to deliver a checkmate.

Queen Sacrifice
A Queen sacrifice is self-explanatory and often regarded as a legendary tactic in order to gain a more favorable position by sacrificing a Queen. In a Queen Sacrifice, the opponent will take the queen, and then, you can follow up with an attack to punish your opponent.
Fastest stalemate (Johan Upmark vs Robin Johansson 1995)
